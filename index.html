My Software History

My first forray into software development began when I was 9 years old. I started learning BASIC on a Radio Shack TRS-80 computer. The pinnacle of my early programming was changing the color of the screen and repeating the same word down the left side. The unit featured arcade game ripoffs which were fun, but also kind of depressing. We couldn't afford to replace the TRS-80 once it died so I put programming aside for decades assuming it was only for upper middle class nerds in California. *sniffle*

From the confines of an Air Force tent while deployed to Helmand Province, Afghanistan in 2012, I casually started reading up on more modern languages like HTML and CSS. Since then, I've used platforms such as Wordpress and Shopify for blogging and online sales. In April 2015, I completed an intensive 12 week JavaScript course at The Iron Yard in Austin, TX where my skills were honed on the afore mentioned languages as well as on associated frameworks: Node, Angular, Backbone and Sails.

A Brief History of THIS GUY!

Growing up, my family regularly moved back and forth among several small towns across Southern Illinois as my Mom cycled between jobs and university semesters. She eventually earned a degree in Latin and Ancient Greek going on to study at a Methodist seminary in Indianapolis. An early appreciation for learning was instilled within both my brother and me as family conversations often revolved around religion, ethics, politics, science and art. Church was a big part of our upbringing and we could just as easily find ourselves attending a Southern Baptist revival as a Society of Friends (Quaker) meeting.

As a result of this emphasis on curiosity and passion, I began to follow my own curiosity and became comfortable with uncertainty due to the constant moving. I earned a bachelor's degree in Music Education from Southern Illinois University, Carbondale after which I played trumpet and sang with the Army Band in Ft. Lewis, WA.

Getting a sense for what a paycheck was all about, I decided to study business earning my MBA at University of Illinois, Champaign/Urbana. After graduation, I became Executive Director of The Mt. Vernon Rescue Mission, worked for an importer in Austin, commissioned as an Air Force Healthcare Administration Officer, started an outdoor apparel company and eventually landed back in Austin to study web development.

I enjoy working with forward thinking leaders, entrepreneurs, designers and engineers.
